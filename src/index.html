Nate Eagleson programs a good bit, plays music semi-seriously, and writes from time to time. He lives in Elizabethtown, Pennsylvania, with his beautiful and talented wife Stephanie and his son James.

Software

Nate makes tools that help real people solve actual problems.

Some software he has written or helped write is on the Web:

  • He wrote the cross-platform wxPython app for Bright Ideas Press's Illuminations curriculum.
  • He was lead developer, primary code reviewer, sysadmin, and all-around problem solver for initial development of 2x30.com (alas, like so many startups, it is no more).
  • Some of his contributions to the world of open source software (and his slowly-evolving .emacs.d) can be found on GitHub.
  • When he first learned JavaScript, he realized the setInterval function implied in-browser games, which resulted in Tetris (albeit a poor one).

He has used several programming languages, most of which are listed below:

  • Python
  • PHP
  • SQL
  • bash
  • JavaScript
  • Java
  • C++
  • Objective-C
  • C
  • Emacs Lisp
  • Scheme

He knows his way around XML, HTML, and CSS, but does not list them as programming languages, since they are not Turing-complete.

He does primarily LAMP web development, but has been caught writing command-line utilities for *nix systems, writing cross-platform GUI apps in Java Swing or wxPython, and on very rare occasions playing with graphics algorithms or video game engines.

He strongly prefers *nix environments - he has used Solaris, OS X, various Linuxes, and FreeBSD, and his desktop is currently OS X. He uses Windows only when software he is writing needs to work on Windows.

Nate maintains some notes on programming, in hopes of passing on what wisdom he has gained during his fourteen years of programming.

Music

Nate plays several instruments with varying degrees of success:

  • piano
  • flute
  • acoustic guitar
  • electric guitar (Telecaster)
  • upright bass (bluegrass-style, and it has been a long while)
  • electric bass (basic rock, and it has been a while)
  • keyboards (strongly preferring his Nord G2X and Nord Stage)

He is okay at synth patch design (which is why he is so intensely fond of the G2X), and tries to use primarily his own patches. He owes most of what he has learned about sound design to the incredible community at electro-music.com.

He uses LilyPond on the rare occasions that he typesets music.
